Clustering Recency & Consistency

  1. Counties with significant values are represented by dots.
  2. Counties are classified as hotspots (red) or coldspots (blue).
  3. The color intensity of colors is determed by the last week it had a significant value.
  4. The size of the county circles are deteremined by the total number of times it was a significant value.
  5. These dots are placed over counties on the map.

Step 1

This page provides a single view representation of the animated choropleth maps by using symbols to show the temporal aspects in a single view. Counties that were either a hot spot or cold spot at least once in 2020 have a circle over them. If the circle is colored blue then the county was a cold spot at some point in time and if it is color red it was a hot spot. The radius of the circle shows the number of times that county was a hot or cold spot in the given period. A circle with a larger radius indicates that it was a hot spot or cold spot more times in the year. A darker color shows the last time the county was a hot spot or cold spot was later in the year while a lighter color means the last time the county was a hot spot or cold spot was earlier in the year.
